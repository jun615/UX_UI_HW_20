    • The Introduction

    Friends of Refugees, founded in 2005, is a non-profit organization dedicated to helping refugees resettle in America. Located in Clarkston, GA, Friends of Refugees' mission is to empower refugees through opportunities that provide for their well-being, education, and employment.

    Over the course of three weeks, our group will be conducting a responsive website redesign to provide stakeholderes, fmailies, and volunteers a clean, easy-to-use website that will evoke a feeling of safety, community, and inclusion.

    The Problem

    We have observed that users, both first-time and long-time volunteers, often struggle to locate pertinent information regarding statistics, funding, or volunteering due to excessive copy or organizational issues, potentially determining their future involvement within that organization.

    The Research

    Interview

    To begin the research process, we started by interviewing users who had experience volunteering with non-profits in the past or interested in volunteering. We asked questions that would help us discover what components of the website would hold the most value to a user. We discovered that the mission of the non-profit was important - as many users wanted validation that the non-profit was truly living out their cause and deliving on their promise.

    Affinity Diagram

    One of the research methods we utilized was the affinity diagram. With the data we collected from the interviews, we were able to group similiar answers to help us draw a bigger picture. This process gave us: greater insight to what our users need out of the website, pointed out the areas of opportunity to make revisions, and guided us throughout the redesign process to enhance the user experience.

    Based off of the different groupings, the team and I brainstormed together to draft up ideas and prepare us for the next step of our research phase.

    User Persona

    With the research conducted, we were able to create our user persona to understand who our user is and to allow us to empathize with them throughout the design process. We gathered some valuable insight which guided us throughout the project and influenced our design process.

    The Iteration

    To Start

    To begin the wireframe, we started with a sketch for the redesign. The three team members each drafted their own sketch of what our new webiste will look like and merged our ideas together. We were able to draw inspiration from the current website as well as what the "competitors" were displaying on their websites.
